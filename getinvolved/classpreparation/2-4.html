Age group 2 - 4

Preparation

Classes for this age group have a duration of around 30 min. Kids of these age groups are very small, they tend to be very energetic and have short attention spans. Therefore, it is not smart to just teach flashcards for the whole duration of the class since, the students can become bored after a period time and wonâ€™t focus. We usually divide the class in three parts e.g. warm-up, teaching and games.

Warm-up

There are many ways to going at warming the students up for class. What volunteers tend to do first is warm up through exercise, click here for an example of warming up. So basically you just do a few exercises like for example: sit-down, stand-up, running, stretches etc. in order to get the students physically warmed up. Being physically active is actually good for the students since physical exercise improves cognitive performance. After some physical exercise the warm up is finalized with singing an easy English song. Click here for a list of some of the songs that our volunteers sing during class. Singing songs are also good because they help kids to memorize words. Warm-up usually takes around 5-7 minutes of the class

Teaching

Teaching usually amounts to around 15 - 20 min of the class. The flash cards are usually taught just by holding up the card, yelling the word out louad and making the students repeat the word. Sometimes it is also helpful to make sounds relating to the words or mimicking the meaning of the words, for example by making chicken sounds when the word chicken is presented (here an example of how flash cards are thaught at New World). Another way is by incorporating movement in teaching by for example clapping, or stomping to the syllables of the word on the flashcard. It's up to you on how you want to teach words to kids, you are free to use your own creative capacities to do so. Most of the times, Vietnamese teachers accompany volunteers to classes in order to help with communication (translation) and support.

Games

After the flash cards are taught to the students, the class is finalized by playing games and a final goodbye song (here is an example). Here you can find a list of games that the volunteers at our center usually play and that work. However you can also feel free to think of your own games. Also, don't be afraid for to ask the Vietnamese teachers for help, usually they know which games to play with the kids.
