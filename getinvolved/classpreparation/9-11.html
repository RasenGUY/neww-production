Age group 9 - 11

Preparation

The majority classes pertaining to this age group usually have a duration of around 45 minutes and are mostly taught in state schools. Even Though, the format is more or less the same as in classes taught in the other age groups. The content that is taught is different.

Warm-up

Normally, you can do some exercise with the students. But in some cases the students will not be responsive to it. You can then just proceed with the class, by for example introducing the students to different ways of greeting, or any of the topic /questions in the list provided by our volunteers (click here for list). It is always good to implement interactivity by for example to using an object (e.g. ball) to throw at students to get them to stand up and participate. Furthermore, next to questions, you can also warm-up by doing some pronunciation exercises ex. Doing tongue twisters or singing songs (click here for and example).

Teaching

Role playing - you can write the word acting or roleplaying on the board and create circle around them. Then you can draw an arrow to the word situation which you have written its definition on the board before hand (you can also let the students themselves explain the definition of the word by going around and asking the students what the word means). Then you can ask the students “where can these situations happen”. You can then brainstorm with the students on different situations that they see everyday and create roleplaying game around a few of the situations that you have chosen. This of course depends on your time limit and the level of English the students are in. The bigger the situation the more students can get involved.

Poems - sometimes you can use some of the vocabulary presented in the English books. The words can be used in classes by for example creating stories, poems or songs.

Word clouds - Use the words from the book (see poems). You can split the class in two teams or three depending on the size of a the classroom. Chose an amount of words each team should finish. Write the words jumbled together with random letters in a cloud pertaining to each word. Each team will be given points depending on the amount of word clouds they solved. The teams have to write down all words in a specified amount of time.

Creativity - Don't be afraid to use your creative juices when designing the structure of your classes. We give you the freedom to take initiave and design the structure of your class according to your own idea (the beauty of teaching English with us).

Games

If there is any time left in your class you can end the class with a game. In many cases students look forward to finalizing the class with a game. We have created a list of the games that many of our volunteers incorporate in there classes (see list).
