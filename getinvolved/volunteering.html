Volunteering

Volunteering in Thai Binh, Vietnam

General Information

So you are thinking about volunteering at the New World International English School ? Here are a few things you should know. Our school is home to around 20 volunteers from all over the world. This is also the maximum number of volunteers our school can accommodate at a time. Yes accommodate, if you decide to volunteer at our school you will be provided accommodation, food and compensation for your help. The compensation is a fixed amount of around 200 USD per month. It is important to note however, that we are not an NGO, we are a social enterprise and thus we pay tax and employ staff. For more detailed information about New World please refer to the about page.

Teaching

Teaching in New World is pretty straight forward. The volunteers of New World are tasked with helping vietnamese students with pronunciation and and getting acquainted to expressing themselves through the use of the English language. Grammar itself is taught by Vietnamese English teachers (some also hired by New World). While it is not your job to teach grammar, you are still allowed to incorporate that in your classes (depending of course the type of classes). Vietnamese teachers and volunteers teach English at the different New World schools across Thai Binh. Next to teaching at the New World schools, the volunteers of New World are also hired by many state schools and some private schools in Thai Binh to teach English to kids. So as a volunteer you may find yourself teaching at either the New World school(s) or at schools in different parts of Thai Binh.

Important to know

There are 4 New World schools spread across Thai Bin. Mrs. Hong is the director of the New World International English School which includes all of New World schools in Thai Binh. Mrs. Hong, her husband and two sons live a short distance

away from Kien Xuong. Mrs. Hong is the go to person for any questions regarding your stay in Thai Binh, anything regarding your visa, your accommodation and also food. Mrs. Hong is also the one who is in charge of the teaching schedule.

At the main school in Thai binh there are teaching aids (staff) which are also coordinators e.g. Mrs. Ly, Ms. Liene, Mrs. Quey and Ms. Huong. Mrs. Ly is like the right hand man of Mrs. Hong, she helps around with a multitude of tasks ranging from

assisting the volunteers with teaching to overseeing planning of some classes. In your stay you will also encounter Mis Ly’s Husband; Duc. He is also the all around helper in the school he does not teach though, he just helps with the maintenance of the school. Ms. Liene is responsible for creating the lesson plans for secondary schools.

Ms. Huong is responsible for creating the lesson plans for primary schools and she does this with the help of Mis Mai who works at the New World English School in Diem Dien. Just like the other teaching staff, Ms. Huong also works as a teaching assistant in some classes taught by volunteers. Finally, Mrs. Quey is responsible for the lesson plans of the kindergartens. Furthermore she also acts as a teaching assistant. It is important to note that the students are taught grammer by Vietnamese teachers, the volunteer's job is to help with vocabulary, pronunciation and more importantly to help the students to become acquainted to interacting through the English language.

The Coordinator/manager of the second school is Ms. Mai. Ms. Mai lives in Vu Thu where she manages the New World school located there. She has two daughters Minh Thu and Thang Thu. Ms. Mai is very hospitable and generous, she will guide you through your classes when you have to teach in Vu Thu. The coordinator/manager of the third school is Mrs. Vanh Anh. Together with Ms. Mai she manages

the New World school in Diem Dien. When teaching at the New World school is Diem Dien, for any questions regarding your lessons, food, drink etc. Mrs. Vanh Anh will be more than happy to help out.

Finally, there is a fourth school in Kien Xuong. This school is managed by Mrs. Huong and Ms. Le. You will also be teaching classes in this school, for any questions regarding your classes you can contact them.

Accomodation

In Thai Binh we have 2 houses, the old house and the new one. The Old House (old school) has 8 bedrooms, spread across 3 floors. There is a Bathroom on every floor with showering facilities. The switch on the outside of each bathroom heats the water in advance. Within 30 mins you will have enough Hot water. The bedrooms are spacious and are each equipped with a wardrobe, bed(pillows & duvet) and a desk. Each Bedroom has a full functioning balcony for relaxing. The old house use to

be the main school were classes were held, however since the beginning of february 2019 it is not used as a school anymore.

The second house is the main building of the New World International school. Most of you will be staying in this building (if there is space left). You will also be giving teaching some of your English classes in this building. The building is a six storey building that houses 6 classrooms, one large kitchen, a balcony on the 5th and 6th floor, one public bathroom on each of the floors except for the 5th and the 6th - On these floors there is one bathroom in the double rooms-, four double rooms - these are shared rooms in which two people can stay-, 4 single bedrooms and one common room on the 4th floor. All of the single rooms are equipped with a king size bed a desk and a wardrobe. All of the double bedrooms have two of each (wardrobe, desk and kingsize bed). Two of the 4 single rooms are spacious rooms and the other two are fairly sized. There is also airconditioning in all of the rooms, but some rooms share on 1 airco (4 rooms in total). There is also a common laundry room, the laundry room has a washing machine but no dryer. So all of the clothes are air/sundried on the 6th and 5th floor on racks.

Food

New World provides food for its volunteers twice a day everyday. Lunch is served around 11:00 am and dinner around 6:30pm everyday. The menu is always vietnamese (meat and vegetarian) dishes, but if you like you can also cook some food for yourself as some volunteers choose to do. If you're a vegetarian, food will be prepared for you as well just let us know beforehand so that the cook takes you into consideration. Food is for the most part eaten together, in the main house (the main school) we eat together and we also clean up together. Sometimes volunteers choose to eat out or eat with the families of their students, this is completely fine, but it is very much appreciated if you let the staff know that you are not going to partake in the meals so that food spillage can be avoided.

Travel

During your stay here in Thai Binh, you will get chances to travel. Travel is usually done during the weekend because that's when all of us are free. You can choose to travel around and explore by yourself. However, sometimes trips are planned by the volunteers. There are many tourist areas like for example Ninh Binh (1,5 h from Thai Binh), Cat Ba (3,5 h from Thai Binh) or Hanoi (2 h from Thai Binh) not to far from Thai Binh. Sometimes we also plan trip with all of the workers of the school. There are many things to see and places to visit in Thai Binh. The school has around three bikes, you can borrow the bikes to go exploring, don't forget to call dibs. Also, you can rent a bike which will cost you around 700 000 VND per month (USD 30). When traveling around be sure to always wear a helmet and drive save. Traffic accidents are common in Vietnam, on average one person dies every hour.

Guidelines

First of all there are some rules of the house you need to follow, if you get accepted to teach at New World. The main rule is respect for and tolerance of others. This rule is not written down, it’s just common sense and a matter of ethics. If you are unable to abide to this rule, be prepared to not have many friends. Another rule of the house is to clean up after yourself, we try to help Mrs. Hong to keep the place clean by helping with washing dishes after eating food. Another thing that you need to consider is saving energy. Electricity is becoming increasingly expensive in Thai binh, therefore we have to be mindful about our energy consumption. Thus, turn lights off if your not using and turn of the water heater when you are finished using it. Also, if you see a some lights on where there is nobody around, turn them off.

Next to house rules, there are also some rules you need to consider when teaching in New World. First of all there is a dress code you have to follow when teaching at state schools. No sleeveless shirts, short pants (over the knee) or slippers, you can wear shirts that have sleeves and pants that are under the knees or long pants. Dresses need to below the knee and preferably worn over some leggings. Other then that, if you are sick you have to let the staff know beforehand so that they can find a replacement for you. If you do not show up for your shift more than three times your contract will be terminated and you will be sent back to your home country.

FAQ

  • Do I need to vaccinate before coming to Vietnam ?

    It’s not legally required at the airport, but we advise you to do so. Talk you doctor and ask what kind of vaccines you for traveling to asia. Vaccinations that you can consider taking are:
    • Adult polio booster
    • Tetanus / diphtheria
    • Hepatitis A
    • Typhoid
    • Yellow Fever

  • Will there be any form of pickup from the airport ?

    Yes, we have a driver that picks up our volunteers from the airport in e.g. in Ha Noi and from there it is a 2 hour drive to the school in Thai Binh

  • Do I need a passport photo ?

    Yes, you need to bring a passport photo with you for immigration and visa purposes. Click here for information about the photos specs.

  • What are the living expenses in Vietnam ?

    It is really cheap living in vietnam, our volunteers usually get by with the 200 USD they receive. Also, food and accomodation is provided any expense outside of this you have to cover yourself. Furthermore, eating out is cheap around 25k-50k VND (1-2 USD)for a complete dish.

  • Do I have to pay for my own ticket ?

    Yes, you fly to us and we pick you up.

  • Do I need to bring my laptop ?

    This is not required, you can get fast internet on your phone in Thai Binh. However, it is useful to have your laptop with you its all up to you. Furthermore all of our schools have wifi which you can use freely.

  • Do I need to bring a sleeping bag, pillows blankets etc. ?

    No, we will provide you with a furnished room, it has a desk a bed with covers and a pillow

  • Are rooms shared ?

    Not all of them, there are 4 rooms in total that are shared, but these rooms are very spacious.

  • Can I cook my own food ?

    Yes we have a big kitchen in our school, you can get fresh vegetables, meats, beans and tofu at the local markets (very cheaply) and use the kitchen to prepare your food.

  • What is the minimum stay ?

    All our applicants are required to stay at least three months

  • Is there a compensation (stipend) ?

    Yes, every month you receive around 200 USD for your help (cash).

  • Can I use my bankcard in Thai Binh ?

    Yes you can there are ATM’s that accept MAESTRO, VISA and MASTERcard's. You can also withdraw money from some stores in Thai Binh.

  • Do I need insurance ?

    Insurance is not a legal requirement. However we do advise you to get insurance you never know what can happen. There are some companies that provide international insurance e.g. https://www.worldnomads.com.

  • How difficult is it to get around in Thai Binh ?

    Fairly easy, there are meter taxies that are quite cheap, Also we have bikes at the school that you can use to move around in the weekends be sure to arrange with other volunteers.

  • Can I receive visitors during my stay at New World ?

    Yes you can, be sure to ask the director so she can arrange for accomodation for your visitors. Keep in mind that you will be working during the week, so the best time for visits is during holidays.
